The Inspiration

“Money doesn’t grow on trees” - parents everywhere
Sure, it’s a cliché saying, but it’s true!

We wanted to teach children (ages 7-12) the value of money. By associating their allowance with the completion of chores, we empower kids to make the connection between their effort and a reward. In addition, parents no longer have to put up with constant pestering about new toys or money. Not only does that make eventually achieving that reward so much more satisfying, but it really shows that it’s never too early to start saving!

Personas

Primary user:
Ava Chang (she/her) is a nine year old who wants to buy an new lego set, but can't convince parents as they recently bought her one. Although she receives an allowance upon completion of her weekly chores, she often spends her income on small toys or junk food. They give an ultimatum: either start saving up her allowance, or no new lego set. Her parents suggest budgeting her money, but she has no idea where to start.

Secondary user:
Thomas Chang (he/him) is Ava’s thirty-nine year old father. He’s tired of Ava constantly asking him for new toys and gizmos and wants her to learn the value of money. Thomas doesn’t want to spend too much time setting up a system for Ava. His ideal product would have an easy-to-use UI, teach Ava how to budget, and be enjoyable for her so she continues to use it.

To build a product that Ava would enjoy using, we needed to create a playful and intuitive UI. It should be fun to use for younger children, but capable enough for pre-teens.

Solution Approach

To create a product that would satisfy both the primary and secondary personas, we created the following requirements for our minimum viable product. The product should:

  1. Enable parents to choose what chores to complete and at what frequency
  2. Have a feature that allows the parent to select the allowance amount and frequency
  3. Have a feauture that allows the child to set goals to save towards

Wireframe

To begin visualizing what our product should look like, I created a hand-sketched wireframe with our critical feautures.


Prototype

Once I had a rough idea of the app layout, I took to Figma to create a prototype.

But first, I wanted to created a friendly character to be the face of our app! I thought that having one character motif would emphasize our fun and light-hearted take on finances. On that note, what better animal to represent our goal than a little piggy (bank)?
