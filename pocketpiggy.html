Personas

Primary user:
Ava Chang (she/her) is a nine year old who wants to buy an new lego set, but can't convince parents as they recently bought her one. Although she receives an allowance upon completion of her weekly chores, she often spends her income on small toys or junk food. They give an ultimatum: either start saving up her allowance, or no new lego set. Her parents suggest budgeting her money, but she has no idea where to start.

Secondary user:
Thomas Chang (he/him) is Ava‚Äôs thirty-nine year old father. He‚Äôs tired of Ava constantly asking him for new toys and gizmos and wants her to learn the value of money. Thomas doesn‚Äôt want to spend too much time setting up a system for Ava. His ideal product would have an easy-to-use UI, teach Ava how to budget, and be enjoyable for her so she continues to use it.

To build a product that Ava would enjoy using, we needed to create a playful and intuitive UI. It should be fun to use for younger children, but capable enough for pre-teens.

Solution Approach

To create a product that would satisfy both the primary and secondary personas, we created the following requirements for our minimum viable product. The product should:

  1. Enable parents to choose what chores to complete and at what frequency
  2. Have a feature that allows the parent to select allowance amount and frequency
  3. Have a feauture that allows the child set goals to save towards
